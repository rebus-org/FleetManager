Data Protection

At rebus.fm, we care about data protection and your rights as a user, and we want to do everything as transparently as possible.

This document describes how we handle data in Fleet Manager (FM) and thus how we adhere to GDPR as well as other laws.

In this document, the concepts "Data Controller" and "Data Processor" are used with the meaning as defined in GDPR.

To contact rebus.fm regarding Data Protection, please direct emails to: mogens@rebus.fm.

If something is not entirely clear, and/or if you believe there might be a problem in relation to any kind of legislation in the country you are in, please get in touch with rebus.fm, and we will make the necessary adjustments.

Types of Data

All data in FM falls into either of two categories:

  • Personal Data
  • Event Data

Personal Data is the data required for you, as a user, to be signed up to FM. It consists of nothing more than

  • a random user ID
  • your email address
  • a salted and hashed password
  • which accounts in FM you can access

Event Data is the technical data forwarded to FM by Rebus endpoints. It can generally be divided into

  • technical data (IDs, metrics, etc.)
  • payload data (message bodies and saga snapshots)

As the technical data only describes various technical aspects of message processing, it is not capable of carrying personal information.

The payload data however, _can be anything_, as its contents are defined by the YOU, as the user of FM, because FM basically accepts this data without knowing what it is.

Roles

In relation to your Personal Data, rebus.fm acts as a "Data Controller".

In relation to Event Data, rebus.fm is a "Data Processor" and YOU (as the legal entity operating FM) are the "Data Controller".

Responsibilities

Personal Data

The aforementioned Personal Data is stored solely to be able to allow users to log in to FM and be authorized to work with specific accounts.

You, as a user, can get in touch with rebus.fm anytime and have the data we have registered about you

  • delivered to your email address (either in plain text or machine-readable form)
  • corrected, should you have encountered inaccurate information
  • irreversibly deleted
within 48 hours after receiving your request.

Event Data

As FM has no way of knowing the contents of the payload data sent to it, YOU as a user assume full responsibility of the data sent to FM.

Should you, as the "Data Controller" be required to comply with YOUR subjects' requests regarding THEIR data, rebus.fm can help by having all of your Event Data within a specific account

  • delivered to you in machine-readable form
  • irreversibly deleted
within 48 hours after receiving your request.
